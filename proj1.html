Tweetfit

Digital Health Hackathon 2015 WTH Prize

TweetFit is a Wep App designed to motivate unfit people to excersize more. It was inspired by Jeff Kenney-Pulver, a man who lost weight by tweeting whenever he was going to excersize and then he felt inclined to do it.
Our wepapp has a simple-to-use UI that includes multiple sporting activites to tweet about, in a few clicks. Certain Tweeting options include requests for collabrative excersize to possibly meet up with some local twitter friends!
It stores your exercise records in a google spreadsheet so you can easily monitor your exzersize!

Basically when one of the certain buttons is pressed a tweet is tweeted to your twitter account saying you will be going to do the sporting activity you selected. It was made over the course of 36 hours during a hackathon using HTML, CSS and Twitter's APIs. It won the WTH prize (What the Health!?) for the best/wackiest idea.
